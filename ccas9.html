北外举办全球区域国别学共同体2025年会

CCAS 2025 Annual Conference Held at BFSU

2025年10月20日 October 20, 2025
GAFSU秘书处 GAFSU Secretariat
3, 678

全球区域国别学共同体2025年年会

全球区域国别学共同体2025年会日前在北京举行。年会由北京外国语大学主办，北京外国语大学区域与全球治理高等研究院承办，北京大学区域与国别研究院协办，联合国驻华系统支持，会议主题为“面向未来的全球治理：《未来契约》与‘和合善治’”，来自40多个国家和地区的200多位知名专家学者参与。

中共中央对外联络部研究室副主任赵静，中国政府友谊奖获得者、联合国教科文组织前总干事、北京外国语大学联合国与国际组织研究中心名誉主任伊琳娜·博科娃，联合国大学日内瓦政策研究中心主任Adam Day，中宣部有关领导，北京大学国际关系学院教授，区域与国别研究院副院长、中国东南亚研究会副会长翟崑，北京外国语大学校长、党委副书记、全球区域国别学共同体秘书长贾文键，党委副书记贾德忠，党委常委、副校长赵刚等出席会议。来自清华大学、北京大学、中国人民大学、复旦大学、中国政法大学、南京大学、普林斯顿大学、伦敦大学亚非学院、波恩大学、根特大学、达卡大学、圣地亚哥大学等四十余所国内外高校领导和学界同仁线上线下参会。

与会嘉宾分别致辞。

赵静表示，希望各位专家为弥合全球治理赤字、探索“和合”理念时代价值和实践路径提供真知灼见。

常启德呼吁各方以“倾听、联结、落实”为准则，共同构建知识驱动、问责明晰的多边未来。

贾文键表示，中国提出的全球治理倡议与中国学者提出的“和合善治”理念为全球治理注入了东方智慧，北外利用外语与区域国别研究深度结合的优势，努力构建区域国别学自主知识体系。

伊琳娜·博科娃、翟崑分别做题为全球治理倡议与全球胜任力、区域国别视角下的全球治理的主旨发言。

博科娃提到，在国际社会面临多重挑战之际，应坚定加强联合国多边体系建设，并将全球胜任力作为高等教育的核心，以培养善于适应世界变局的青年人才。

翟崑指出，中国区域国别学以具体国家与区域的综合战略性研究为基石，遵循“学科、人才、智库”三位一体建设发展路径，旨在培养复合型人才，为全球治理提供关键人才支撑。

贾文键、伊琳娜·博科娃共同为“区域国别研究交叉学科创新引智基地”揭牌，《大学实施〈未来契约〉行动计划》、BFSU-SOAS关于区域国别学的联合倡议、“和合善治”青年学者网络倡议三项共同体建设成果在年会主旨论坛上发布。

年会开幕式由北外党委常委、副校长赵刚主持。

主旨发言环节由北京外国语大学区域与全球治理高等研究院党总支书记、院长刘铁娃主持。成果发布环节由北京外国语大学区域与全球治理高等研究院常务副院长王展鹏主持。

政府相关部门领导、高校师生等参加大会。

本次年会下设六个平行分论坛。

分论坛一以“四大倡议与《未来契约》治理路径”为主题，与会专家围绕四大全球倡议与人类命运共同体建设进行深入研讨。

分论坛二以“区域国别学学科体系建设”为主题，围绕“期刊赋能”与“融合创新”两大主题进行研讨。北京外国语大学党委常委、副校长赵刚教授为分论坛致欢迎词。

分论坛三以“万隆精神与全球南方团结”为主题，紧扣“万隆精神传承”核心，分设南亚问题、东南亚研究、非洲与拉美议题三节进行专题研讨。

分论坛四以“多极化时代的大国责任”为主题。美国场围绕“美国向何处去？”和“特朗普2.0以来的中美关系及走向”两个主题进行研讨；欧洲场围绕多极化格局、理性认知与良性互动等议题展开交流。

分论坛五以“政党政治与全球治理创新”为主题，围绕世界政党格局与全球治理走向、政党驱动体系逻辑与国家社会治理创新、政党在国际合作中的功能创新等议题展开研讨。北京外国语大学党委副书记贾德忠教授出席分论坛并主持主旨发言环节。

分论坛六以“可持续发展中的青年使命”为主题，进行六大具体议题下的前沿性学术研究报告，北京外国语大学区域与全球治理高等研究院副院长梁宗正主持“四大倡议与《未来契约》治理路径”议题主旨发言。报告结束后，北京外国语大学区域与全球治理高等研究院副书记崔玉晶为本次论坛获奖论文颁奖。

10月18日上午，论坛闭幕。北京外国语大学区域与全球治理高等研究院常务副院长王展鹏教授发表闭幕致辞。

与会中外专家学者一致认为，全球区域国别学共同体应顺应时代发展，瞄准前沿议题，共同推动区域国别学学科前瞻性发展。

2025 CCAS Annual Conference Held at BFSU

The 2025 Annual Conference of the Consortium for Country and Area Studies (CCAS) was recently held in Beijing. The Conference was hosted by Beijing Foreign Studies University (BFSU), organized by the Academy of Area and Global Governance of BFSU, co-organized by the Institute of Area and Country Studies, Peking University, and supported by the United Nations System in China. Centered on the theme “Future-Oriented Global Governance: The Pact for the Future and ‘Harmonious Unity and Good Governance,’” the Conference brought together more than 200 renowned experts and scholars from over 40 countries and regions.

Attendees included Zhao Jing, Deputy Director of the Research Office of the International Department of the CPC Central Committee; Irina Bokova, recipient of the Chinese Government Friendship Award, former Director-General of UNESCO, and Honorary Director of the Center for UN and International Organization Studies at BFSU; Adam Day, Director of the United Nations University Centre for Policy Research (Geneva); relevant leaders from the Publicity Department of the CPC Central Committee; Zhai Kun, Professor at the School of International Studies, Peking University, Deputy Director of the Institute of Area and Country Studies, and Vice President of the China Society for Southeast Asian Studies; Jia Wenjian, President and Deputy Party Secretary of BFSU and Secretary-General of CCAS; Jia Dezhong, Deputy Party Secretary of BFSU; and Zhao Gang, Member of the Standing Committee of the Party Committee and Vice President of BFSU. Leaders and scholars from more than forty universities and institutions—including Tsinghua University, Peking University, Renmin University of China, Fudan University, China University of Political Science and Law, Nanjing University, Princeton University, SOAS University of London, the University of Bonn, Ghent University, the University of Dhaka, and the University of San Diego—participated both online and in person.

Distinguished guests delivered opening remarks.

At the international academic symposium, Zhao Jing expressed the hope that experts would offer insights to help bridge global governance deficits and explore the contemporary value and practical pathways of the concept of “harmonious unity.”

Chang Qide called on all parties to adopt “listening, connecting, and implementation” as guiding principles to jointly build a knowledge-driven and clearly accountable multilateral future.

Jia Wenjian noted that China’s global governance initiatives, together with the concept of “harmonious unity and good governance” advanced by Chinese scholars, have infused global governance with Eastern wisdom. Leveraging BFSU’s strengths in integrating foreign language studies with country and area studies, the University is committed to building an autonomous knowledge system for the discipline.

Irina Bokova and Zhai Kun delivered keynote speeches titled Global Governance Initiatives and Global Competence and Global Governance from the Perspective of Country and Area Studies, respectively.

Bokova emphasized that amid multiple challenges confronting the international community, it is essential to strengthen the UN-centered multilateral system and to make global competence a core component of higher education in order to cultivate young talents capable of navigating global transformations.

Zhai Kun pointed out that China’s country and area studies are grounded in comprehensive, strategic research on specific countries and regions, following a “discipline–talent–think tank” integrated development pathway, with the aim of cultivating interdisciplinary professionals to provide critical human capital for global governance.

Jia Wenjian and Irina Bokova jointly unveiled the Interdisciplinary Innovation and Talent Introduction Base for Country and Area Studies. Three CCAS initiatives were released at the plenary forum: the University Action Plan for Implementing the Pact for the Future; the BFSU–SOAS Joint Initiative on Country and Area Studies; and the Youth Scholars Network Initiative on Harmonious Unity and Good Governance.

The opening ceremony was chaired by Zhao Gang, Vice President of BFSU.

The keynote session was chaired by Liu Tiewa, Party Secretary and Dean of the Academy of Area and Global Governance, BFSU. The outcomes release session was chaired by Wang Zhanpeng, Executive Vice Dean of the Academy.

Leaders from relevant government departments, as well as faculty members and students from universities, also attended the Conference.

The Conference featured six parallel subforums:

Subforum I, themed The Four Global Initiatives and Governance Pathways of the Pact for the Future, engaged experts in in-depth discussions on the Four Global Initiatives and the building of a community with a shared future for mankind.

Subforum II, themed Building the Disciplinary System of Country and Area Studies, focused on “journal empowerment” and “integrative innovation.” Vice President Zhao Gang delivered a welcome address.

Subforum III, themed The Bandung Spirit and Solidarity of the Global South, centered on the legacy of the Bandung Spirit, with sessions on South Asia, Southeast Asian studies, and issues concerning Africa and Latin America.

Subforum IV, themed Responsibilities of Major Powers in a Multipolar Era, featured a U.S. session discussing “Where Is the United States Heading?” and “China–U.S. Relations Since Trump 2.0 and Their Trajectory,” and a Europe session addressing multipolar dynamics, rational perceptions, and constructive interaction.

Subforum V, themed Political Parties and Innovation in Global Governance, examined the global party landscape and the direction of global governance, party-driven system logics and innovations in state–society governance, and functional innovation of political parties in international cooperation. Jia Dezhong, Deputy Party Secretary of BFSU, attended and chaired the keynote segment.

Subforum VI, themed Youth Mandate in Sustainable Development, presented frontier research reports across six specific topics. Liang Zongzheng, Vice Dean of the Academy, chaired the keynote session on The Four Global Initiatives and Governance Pathways of the Pact for the Future. Following the presentations, Cui Yujing, Deputy Party Secretary of the Academy, presented awards to outstanding papers.

On the morning of October 18, the Conference concluded. Professor Wang Zhanpeng, Executive Vice Dean of the Academy of Area and Global Governance, delivered the closing remarks.

Participating Chinese and international scholars unanimously agreed that the Consortium for Country and Area Studies should keep pace with the times, focus on frontier issues, and jointly advance the forward-looking development of the discipline.
