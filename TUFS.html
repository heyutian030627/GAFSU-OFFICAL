学校简介

东京外国语大学（日文：とうきょうがいこくごだいがく；英文：Tokyo University of Foreign Studies，简称TUFS）是一所以外语教育和国际研究为特色的国立大学，位于日本东京都。学校于1897年正式成立，并于1949年开设大学教育，是日本“超级国际化大学计划”成员之一。
TUFS的历史可追溯至江户幕府于1857年设立的蕃书调所（政府翻译中心），是日本最早的外国研究机构。明治时代，该机构并入东京大学外国语学部，并在1873年发展为东京外国语学校。随后，学校与东京商业学校（现一桥大学）合并，后于1897年再次独立，确立了作为外国语高等教育机构的地位。
TUFS秉持校训Language Is the Gateway to the World（语言是通往世界的门户），致力于培养具备多语言能力和跨文化理解的国际化人才。学校设有学部及大学院，涵盖语言文化、国际社会、区域研究等领域。此外，TUFS与世界100多个国家的180多所大学建立了合作关系，是日本国际交流与外国语教育的核心学术机构之一。

About the University

Tokyo University of Foreign Studies (Japanese: とうきょうがいこくごだいがく; English: Tokyo University of Foreign Studies, abbreviated as TUFS) is a national university specializing in foreign language education and international studies, located in Tokyo, Japan. The university was formally established in 1897 and began offering university-level education in 1949. It is one of the participating institutions in Japan’s “Top Global University Project.”
The history of TUFS can be traced back to the Bansho Shirabesho (Institute for the Study of Foreign Books), a government translation and research center established by the Edo shogunate in 1857, making it one of Japan’s earliest institutions for foreign studies. During the Meiji era, the institute was incorporated into the School of Foreign Studies of the University of Tokyo and later developed into the Tokyo School of Foreign Languages in 1873. The school subsequently merged with the Tokyo Higher Commercial School (now Hitotsubashi University) before regaining independence in 1897, thereby establishing its status as a higher education institution specializing in foreign languages.
Guided by its motto, “Language Is the Gateway to the World,” TUFS is committed to cultivating internationally minded professionals with strong multilingual competence and cross-cultural understanding. The university comprises undergraduate faculties and graduate schools covering fields such as language and culture, international society, and area studies. In addition, TUFS has established cooperative partnerships with more than 180 universities in over 100 countries, making it one of Japan’s core academic institutions for international exchange and foreign language education.
